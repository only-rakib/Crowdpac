Learn how you can promote your cause on Crowdpac

Crowdpac’s innovative fundraising campaigns give you the power to make meaningful change. From encouraging a member of Congress to listen to their constituents to drafting your PTA president to run for school board, you have the power to take your activism to the next level while inspiring the next generation of changemakers.

Support

Hit the ground running by raising funds directly for a candidate or cause that you feel strongly about.

Whether it's crowdfunding your own candidacy, supporting a slate of candidates and organizations, or selling merchandise to meet a campaign milestone, you can raise the funds you need to reach your fundraising goals.

Start a Support Campaign

Explore

Crowdpac’s Explore tools allow anyone to test a run for office before declaring their candidacy.

It could be a local, state or federal seat, even President; explore campaigns give you the opportunity to decide if you’re ready to run. Test the waters and your financial viability as a candidate before officially throwing your hat into the ring. Supporters’ cards will only be charged if and when you choose to declare.

Start an Explore Campaign

Draft

Encourage someone to run for office by starting a draft campaign to show them they’ll have donors-in-waiting if and when they enter the race.

Whether you’re drafting Stacey Abrams to run for president or your PTA president to run for school board, a draft campaign shows your dream candidate that they’ll have support if they choose to run. Supporters’ pledges convert to donations if the potential candidate chooses to declare their candidacy.

Start a Draft Campaign

Oppose

Raise funds against a politician who you believe to be on the wrong side of an issue and in support of their next challenger.

Opposition funds allow you to send a message: we’re ready for your future opponent, whoever they may be. Whether it’s for the President, a member of Congress or a locally elected official, you have the ability to say enough is enough.

Start an Opposition Campaign

Pressure

Pressure campaigns give people the power to challenge an institution.

They force an elected official to look in their constituents’ direction rather than a lobbyist or party leader. They give students the platform to advocate for their rights at a university. Whatever the cause, pressure campaigns give a voice to those that are left unheard. You collect pledges that convert to donations if the conditions of the campaign go unmet.

Start a Pressure Campaign

Match

Donors love a challenge. Give them one under a specific condition: if they meet a certain dollar amount or goal in a set amount of time, their pledges will be matched and convert to donations.

Our team will help you make sure that your matching campaign is set within the guidelines of the conditions to be met.

See how our Innovative Fundraising tools helped these campaigns

Draft

A “Draft State Senator Jennifer Wexton” campaign went viral on Crowdpac after donors made financial pledges urging Wexton to take on Representative Barbara Comstock for Virginia’s 10th Congressional District. Less than 24 hours after Wexton officially declared her candidacy, the district went from Lean Republican to Toss Up, and she went on to flip a GOP stronghold.

Explore

Before she was the youngest black woman to ever serve in Congress, Lauren Underwood was a registered nurse ready to take on her Republican Representative Randy Hultgren, who broke his promise and voted for the GOP healthcare plan. She raised close to $28,000 in pledges before officially entering the race.

Oppose

After Senator Chuck Grassley’s behavior during the Christine Blassey-Ford testimony, activist Ady Barkan and the Be a Hero team started an opposition fund with a clear message: “Will you fight back against rape culture in the Senate by contributing to fund Senator Chuck Grassley’s future Democratic opponent NOW, and help us match his $1,078,202 from lobbyists?”
